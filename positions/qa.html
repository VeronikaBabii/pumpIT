Junior QA Engineer

Home Positions Junior QA Engineer

A QA (Software Testing and Quality Assurance) or tester is a software quality assurance specialist. A tester is a lot like an investigator or detective. He follows in hot pursuit of a programmer and looks for bugs, uses various deductive methods and hidden techniques. Without thorough testing, it is impossible to achieve high quality software product - that is why QA-specialists are in great demand in IT-companies engaged in development.

All testers can be divided into 2 large groups according to their level of training - Manual QA Engineer and Automation QA Engineer:

Manual QA Engineer or manual tester is an engineer who focuses attention on software development processes, improves them, and prevents defects and problems. All work processes go through "manually": he plans the testing process, writes test cases, identifies problem areas, enters the received data into the database, conducts re-tests of errors after revision by programmers. A QA engineer analyzes the testing process for further optimization.

Automation QA Engineer is a specialist who uses software tools to create tests and check the results of execution. The main task of a QA automator is to create automatic scripts that will check the operation of the program based on test cases written by QA manuals. This helps to reduce the time for testing routine tasks and simplify the whole process.

Who is a QA engineer (tester)?

This is a specialist who conducts software testing in order to detect errors and vulnerabilities for their further correction by programmers. By their work, testers make a valuable contribution to improving the quality of the final software.

Among the responsibilities:

- analysis and clarification of requirements from the customer or business analysts;
- planning the testing process;
- writing test scripts;
- directly testing the software product;
- identification of problem areas, their documentation;
- use of bug tracking systems (bug tracking);
- discussion of fixes with developers, active interaction with them;
- tracking the life cycle of errors;
- retesting of corrected defects;
- testing analysis, its optimization;
- maintenance of test documentation.

A QA specialist should know:

- stages of the software life cycle;
- fundamentals, methods, types and types of testing + the ability to apply this knowledge in practice;
- bug tracking systems (Jira / YouTrack);
- web technologies (HTTP, DOM, JSON, cookie, session, etc.);
- basic principles of OOP and knowledge of SQL;
- how to maintain test documentation;
- Agile / SCRUM methodology;
- English is not lower than Intermediate;
- other assistive technologies.
