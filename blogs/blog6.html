Job search strategy

  • 16th October, 2020
  • Unknown

Finding a job is also a job! Therefore it is necessary to treat it accordingly - with all seriousness. It is necessary to set a goal, or rather to define clear requirements or at least wishes for the future workplace and set priorities. Sending resumes to a thousand vacancies and waiting for feedback is not the work strategy itself. It will be more effective to make a list of companies in which you would like to get a job as a programmer. Approximate action plan:

  1. Using LinkedIn or hh.ru, find a suitable IT company, find an hr-manager in the list of employees and send him your resume.

  2. Fill in your profile 100%.

  3. In addition to a well-designed resume, send a cover letter.

Go beyond the Internet - participate in conferences, workshops, etc. This is an opportunity to get "useful" acquaintances and learn about vacancies that are not published on the Internet.

Summary: how to properly design and "sell"

The first thing a recruiter will pay attention to is the resume format. It is unknown on which device it will view it, so it is better to take care of versatility and send a resume in pdf format to save all gaps and indents. Size also matters:

  1. For junior - 1 or 1.5 pages;

  2. For middle - 2 pages;

  3. For a senior - no more than 3 pages. Focus on recent years.

But always the most important thing is the content. If you already have relevant experience and this is not the start of a career in it for you, highlight the main thing - describe real projects with personal achievements. The more specific, the better, give clear facts and figures. It is more difficult for junior or a student to write a resume. It is not necessary to paint "non-IT" work experience - it is better to focus on education, completed courses, read books and attended it-events, etc. A good solution would be to write a cover letter and provide a portfolio.

Interview: how to survive?

To pass the interview successfully, the programmer needs to prepare. Preliminary training depends on the position you are applying for, but there are points that you should know in advance in any case.

  • Learn as much as you can about the company to understand the corporate culture and values.

  • Carefully read the requirements for the vacancy and try them on yourself, make a complete picture of what you will do if you are hired.

  • Refresh your knowledge of basic technical issues. Most often, questions in a technical interview start with the basics. Get ready for them!

It is more difficult to pass an interview without experience. But it may seem so out of excitement. If you honestly wrote about it in the resume, the interviewer will take this point into account.

General tips:

  • Don't be afraid of big companies.

  • Focus on the benefits you can bring to the company.

  • Learn English - it will definitely give you an advantage.

  • Be worthy of the job you want to get!
